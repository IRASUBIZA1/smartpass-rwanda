Chapter 1: The Forgotten Map

The morning sun rose above the green hills of Kivumu village, spreading golden light across the small homes and fields. Birds sang from the trees while students walked along the dusty road carrying their school bags.

Among them was Ethan, a fourteen-year-old student who always felt different from others.

Ethan was not the strongest student in his class. He was not the richest. He was not the most popular.

But he had something special.

Curiosity.

While other students spent their free time playing games, Ethan loved reading old books, asking questions, and discovering stories about forgotten places.

Some students laughed at him.

"Why do you waste your time searching for things that don't exist?" they asked.

Ethan always smiled and replied:

"Sometimes the things people forget are the things worth discovering."

One afternoon after classes ended, Ethan stayed behind at the school library. The library was old and quiet. Dust covered many books because few students ever opened them.

While searching through the shelves, Ethan noticed a strange book hidden behind several old textbooks.

The book had no title.

Its cover was dark brown, and a mysterious symbol was drawn on it.

Curious, Ethan opened the book slowly.

Inside were drawings, strange symbols, and stories about a place called:

The Hidden Valley of Amani

The book explained that the valley was once a place where wise people protected knowledge that could help communities and future generations.

But many years ago, the valley disappeared, and nobody knew where it was located.

As Ethan turned the final pages, something fell from the book.

It was an old piece of paper.

A map.

The map showed mountains, rivers, and a path leading to a place near Ethan's own village.

Ethan's eyes widened.

"This cannot be real..." he whispered.

Suddenly, he heard footsteps coming closer.

Someone was entering the library.

Ethan quickly closed the book and looked toward the door.

Standing there was Maya, one of the smartest students in his class.

"What are you hiding?" Maya asked with curiosity.

"Nothing," Ethan replied.

Maya smiled.

"You are not good at hiding secrets."

Ethan slowly opened the book and showed her the map.

For a moment, Maya said nothing.

Then she looked at Ethan seriously.

"Ethan... do you know what this means?"

"What?"

"This might not just be an old story."

The two friends stared at the mysterious map.

They did not know it yet, but this discovery would change their lives forever.

Because some secrets are not meant to stay hidden.

And every great journey begins with a single step.
