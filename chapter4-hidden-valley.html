Chapter 4: The Ancient School

Ethan and Maya stood silently inside the hidden passage. The strange lights on the walls revealed drawings of people learning, creating, and sharing knowledge.

The voice they heard earlier came again.

"Who are you, and why have you come to the Hidden Valley of Amani?"

From the shadows appeared an old man wearing a long brown coat. His eyes were calm, but his face showed years of wisdom.

"My name is Elder Kato," he said. "I am one of the guardians of this valley."

Maya looked surprised.

"Guardians? We thought this place was only a legend."

Elder Kato smiled.

"Many important things become legends when people stop believing in them."

He asked Ethan to show him the map.

When Elder Kato saw it, his expression changed.

"This map has not been seen for many years."

"Why did it lead us here?" Ethan asked.

The old guardian looked at him carefully.

"Maybe the valley chose you."

Ethan was confused.

"I am just a normal student."

Elder Kato smiled.

"The greatest people often begin by believing they are ordinary."

The guardian then led them deeper into the valley.

Soon, they reached a place that amazed them.

It was an ancient school.

There were classrooms carved into stone, huge libraries filled with forgotten books, and inventions created by people from the past.

"This was a school where people learned not only information, but how to use knowledge to help others," Elder Kato explained.

Maya looked around with excitement.

"Why did people leave this place?"

The guardian became serious.

"Because some people wanted knowledge only for power. They forgot that true knowledge exists to serve others."

Ethan remembered the words from the map:

"The Valley of Amani protects knowledge of those who seek wisdom, not power."

Elder Kato gave Ethan an old notebook.

"Inside this notebook are lessons from the greatest thinkers of this valley."

Ethan opened it carefully.

The first page contained one sentence:

"A great mind is not measured by what it knows, but by how it helps others."

Ethan looked at Maya.

They both realized their journey was not just about finding a hidden place.

It was about discovering a purpose.

But far away from the valley, the mysterious stranger was getting closer.

He wanted the knowledge of Amani for himself.
