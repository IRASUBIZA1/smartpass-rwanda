📖 The Last Seat in Class

Chapter 2: The Boy Nobody Noticed

The first week at Green Valley Secondary School was not easy for Samuel.

Every morning, he arrived early, cleaned his desk, and prepared his books before the lesson started.

But most students still did not notice him.

To them, Samuel was just the quiet boy who sat at the back of the classroom.

During breaks, groups of students gathered together to share stories and jokes.

Samuel usually stayed near the classroom window, reading his books.

Some students thought he was lonely because he had no friends.

But the truth was different.

Samuel enjoyed learning.

Books were his closest friends because they opened a world beyond his village.

One day, during a mathematics lesson, the teacher wrote a difficult problem on the board.

"Who can solve this?" the teacher asked.

The classroom became silent.

Many students looked away because the question seemed too difficult.

The teacher waited for someone to answer.

Then Samuel slowly raised his hand.

The whole class turned to look at him.

Even the teacher looked surprised.

"Samuel, you can try," the teacher said.

Samuel stood up and walked to the board.

Some students whispered:

"Does he really know the answer?"

"Maybe he is just trying."

Samuel ignored the voices.

He carefully explained every step of the solution.

When he finished, the classroom became quiet.

The teacher smiled.

"Excellent work, Samuel. Your answer is completely correct."

For the first time, students looked at him differently.

They realized that the boy sitting at the back was not ordinary.

He was talented.

But Samuel did not celebrate.

He simply returned to his seat and continued studying.

That day, a small change began.

The boy nobody noticed was slowly becoming impossible to ignore.

However, Samuel did not know that his biggest challenges were still ahead.
