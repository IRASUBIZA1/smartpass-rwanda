Chapter 4: A Second Chance

One morning, while Linka was washing clothes outside, a visitor arrived at the village.

It was one of her former teachers.

The teacher had never forgotten Linka's intelligence and determination.

After learning that she had dropped out of school, he became concerned and decided to visit her.

"Linka was one of our brightest students," he told her aunt. "She has been awarded a full scholarship."

Linka could hardly believe what she was hearing.

A full scholarship meant that her tuition, books, and other school expenses would be covered.

For the first time in many months, she felt hope returning to her heart.

That evening, she carefully prepared her school materials and dreamed about returning to class.

A few days later, Linka finally went back to school.

She was happy to learn again and quickly became one of the best students in her class.

However, not everyone was pleased with her success.

Her aunt grew increasingly unhappy because Linka now spent less time doing household work.

The tension in the house continued to grow.

Linka did not know that another difficult challenge was waiting for her.

The second chance she had received would soon be tested.
