Chapter 3: The Old Family Conflict

After discovering their family connection, Amina and David decided to find out why their families had been separated for so many years.

They knew that anger from the past was controlling the present, but nobody had ever explained the real reason.

Amina went home and asked her grandmother about the conflict.

"Grandmother, why do our families dislike each other?"

The old woman became quiet.

She looked toward the window where the mango tree could be seen in the distance.

"Some stories are painful to remember," she said.

"But maybe it is time the truth is finally known."

Many years ago, before Amina and David were born, their families were close friends.

They worked together, helped each other, and lived peacefully.

But one day, a valuable family document disappeared.

Each family blamed the other.

The friendship turned into anger.

Over the years, children grew up hearing only one side of the story.

The disagreement became a tradition of hatred.

David also learned the story from his father.

"We were told they betrayed us," his father explained.

"But nobody ever proved what happened."

David became confused.

"If nobody knows the truth, why have we continued fighting?"

His father had no answer.

That night, Amina and David met secretly under the mango tree.

"The conflict started because people believed a story without searching for the truth," Amina said.

David nodded.

"Then we need to find what really happened."

They made a promise:

"We will discover the truth and bring peace, even if it is difficult."

But they did not know that the answer was hidden somewhere nobody had searched for many years.

A forgotten letter.

A lost memory.

And a secret that could change everything.
