Chapter 5: The Test Of Courage

The next morning, Ethan and Maya woke up inside the ancient school of Amani. They had discovered a place filled with knowledge, but Elder Kato told them that they had not yet completed their journey.

"Anyone who enters the valley must pass the Test of Courage," Elder Kato explained.

Maya looked curious.

"What kind of test is it?"

The old guardian smiled.

"It is not a test of strength. It is a test of the heart."

Elder Kato led them to a large stone door at the end of the valley. On the door were three words:

Truth. Courage. Kindness.

"The door will only open when you understand their meaning," he said.

Ethan touched the first symbol.

A voice echoed through the room.

"What is truth?"

Ethan thought carefully.

"Truth is accepting reality, even when it is difficult."

The first symbol began to glow.

Then Maya stepped forward.

The second symbol asked:

"What is courage?"

Maya looked at Ethan.

"Courage is not being without fear. Courage is moving forward even when you are afraid."

The second symbol started glowing.

Finally, the last symbol asked:

"What is kindness?"

Ethan and Maya looked at each other.

Ethan answered:

"Kindness is using what we have to make life better for others."

The entire door began shining.

Slowly, it opened.

Behind the door was a hidden room filled with ancient inventions, books, and a mysterious crystal placed in the center.

"What is that?" Maya asked.

Elder Kato looked serious.

"That is the Heart of Amani."

"The Heart of Amani contains the knowledge and wisdom collected by generations of guardians."

Ethan stepped closer.

"Can it give someone power?"

The guardian shook his head.

"No. It only strengthens the intentions of the person who uses it. A good heart will create good things. A selfish heart will create destruction."

Suddenly, a loud sound came from outside.

The valley walls shook.

Elder Kato looked worried.

"They have found us."

Ethan and Maya turned toward the entrance.

The mysterious stranger had arrived.

And he wanted the Heart of Amani.
