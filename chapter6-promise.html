Chapter 6: The Plan To Bring Peace

Amina and David knew that words alone would not change the hearts of their families. They needed to show everyone that peace was possible.

They decided to organize a community gathering under the old mango tree where their friendship had started.

"The tree has witnessed the beginning of our story," Amina said. "Maybe it can also witness the beginning of peace."

They invited students, elders, and members of both families.

At first, many people refused to attend.

Some said:

"Why should we listen to the other family?"

Others believed that the old conflict should never be discussed again.

But Amina and David continued preparing.

They collected stories from older people who remembered the time before the conflict.

They discovered that both families once helped each other during difficult times.

Slowly, people began to remember something important:

Before the anger, there had been friendship.

On the day of the gathering, the village became quiet.

Amina stood under the mango tree holding the old letter.

"We cannot change what happened many years ago," she said.

"But we can choose what happens next."

David continued:

"Our families taught us to protect their stories. Today, we want to protect the truth."

Some people listened carefully.

Others still looked uncertain.

Then an elderly woman from the village stood up.

"I remember when these families were friends," she said.

"We lost many years because nobody was brave enough to forgive."

The crowd became silent.

For the first time in many years, both families sat together and listened.

But just when hope began growing, someone interrupted the meeting.

"This changes nothing!" a voice shouted.

Everyone turned around.

A person who had protected the old conflict for years had arrived.

And he was not ready to let peace happen.
