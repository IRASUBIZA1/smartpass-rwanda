Chapter 8: The Weight of Expectations

Success brought Samuel new respect at school, but it also brought new responsibilities.

Many people started expecting more from him.

Teachers believed he could achieve great things.

Students looked up to him.

His family hoped that his education would change their future.

Samuel was proud of his progress, but sometimes he felt the pressure becoming heavier.

One evening after school, Samuel returned home later than usual.

His father was sitting outside their small house, looking worried.

"Samuel, everything is becoming more expensive," his father said.

"We are doing our best, but supporting your education is not easy."

Samuel became silent.

He knew how much his parents sacrificed for him.

He remembered the days when his mother skipped meals so he could have enough food before school.

He remembered his father's tired hands after working all day on the farm.

That night, Samuel looked at his school books.

For the first time, he wondered:

"Am I asking too much from my family?"

The next morning, Samuel was quieter than usual.

Kevin noticed.

"You don't look like yourself today. What happened?"

Samuel explained the situation.

Kevin listened carefully.

"Samuel, your family is not sacrificing because they see you as a burden. They are doing it because they believe in you."

Samuel looked at his friend.

"But what if I fail?"

Kevin smiled.

"Failure is not trying and giving up. You are already winning because you refuse to stop."

Those words gave Samuel new strength.

He understood that carrying expectations did not mean carrying them alone.

There were people who believed in him.

There were people who wanted to see him succeed.

Samuel returned to his studies with a new determination.

He promised himself that he would work hard, not only for his own dreams, but also for everyone who supported him.

However, a new opportunity was about to arrive at Green Valley Secondary School.

An opportunity that could change Samuel's life forever.
