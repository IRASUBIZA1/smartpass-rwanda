📖 The Last Seat in Class

Chapter 1: The New Student at the Back Row

The morning sun slowly appeared above the hills, spreading golden light across the small village.

For most people, it was just another ordinary day.

But for Samuel, it was a day he would never forget.

He stood outside the gate of Green Valley Secondary School, looking at the large buildings in front of him.

Students walked through the gate laughing, talking, and sharing stories about their holidays.

Samuel held his old school bag tightly.

The bag was not new. It had survived many years of studying, but his mother always repaired it carefully whenever it became damaged.

His uniform was clean, although its colour had faded after many years of washing.

His shoes had small repairs, but they were still strong enough to carry him forward.

Samuel looked at the school and smiled.

"This is a new beginning," he whispered.

For years, Samuel had dreamed of studying at this school.

Coming from a small village, he knew education was his greatest opportunity.

His father was a farmer, and his mother sold vegetables at the local market.

They worked hard every day so Samuel could continue his studies.

They did not have much money, but they gave him something more valuable:

Hope.

Before leaving home, his mother held his hand and said:

"Samuel, never be ashamed of where you come from. A person's value is not measured by what they own, but by what they carry in their heart."

Those words stayed in his mind as he entered the classroom.

The classroom was already full of students.

Some were talking about holidays, new clothes, phones, and expensive items.

When Samuel entered, several conversations became quieter.

A few students looked at his old bag and simple uniform.

"Is he joining our class?" one student whispered.

"He looks like he came from a village far away," another replied.

Samuel heard them but decided to stay quiet.

The teacher entered the classroom.

"We have a new student today. His name is Samuel. Please welcome him."

The class gave a small applause.

"Samuel, find yourself a seat."

Samuel looked around.

Most seats were already taken.

The only empty place was at the back.

"There is one seat remaining. You can sit there," the teacher said.

Samuel walked slowly across the classroom.

He placed his bag on the desk and sat down.

The last seat.

The seat nobody wanted.

From that day, many students knew him as:

The boy from the last seat.

But they did not know the truth.

Behind that quiet student was a brilliant mind.

Behind that old school bag was a dream.

And behind that simple appearance was someone who would one day surprise everyone.

Because sometimes...

The person sitting at the back is the one with the biggest future.
