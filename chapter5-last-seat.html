📖 The Last Seat in Class

Chapter 5: The Competition

The day of the academic competition finally arrived.

Students from different classes gathered in the school hall. The atmosphere was full of excitement and nervousness.

Everyone wanted to represent Green Valley Secondary School and prove their abilities.

Samuel sat quietly among the other participants.

Around him were students who had been preparing for months. Some had private tutors, expensive books, and modern learning tools.

Samuel only had his old notebooks and the knowledge he had gained through hard work.

But he believed that determination could compete with any advantage.

The competition began with mathematics.

The first questions were simple, but as the rounds continued, they became more difficult.

Many students started making mistakes.

Samuel remained calm and carefully analyzed each question.

He remembered all the evenings he spent studying under a small lamp at home.

He remembered his parents' sacrifices.

He knew he could not give up.

After mathematics came science.

This was the subject many students feared.

One question required students to explain a scientific problem using creativity and knowledge.

Samuel thought carefully before writing his answer.

Instead of only giving a textbook explanation, he connected the answer to problems faced by farmers in his village.

The judges were impressed by his approach.

"He does not only memorize information. He understands how knowledge can solve real problems," one judge said.

When the final results were announced, the hall became silent.

The head teacher stepped forward with the results.

"In third place..."

"In second place..."

Everyone waited for the final name.

"The winner of this year's academic competition is..."

"Samuel."

The hall filled with applause.

Students who once ignored him were now standing and celebrating.

Samuel looked around, almost unable to believe what had happened.

The boy who had started at the last seat had achieved something nobody expected.

But Samuel knew this was not the end.

It was only the beginning of a bigger journey.

After the ceremony, Kevin approached him.

For the first time, he did not look at Samuel with pride or judgment.

He looked at him with respect.

"Congratulations, Samuel," Kevin said.

Samuel smiled.

"Thank you."

A small friendship had started.

And the classroom would never see the last seat the same way again.
