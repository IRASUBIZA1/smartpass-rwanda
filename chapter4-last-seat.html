📖 The Last Seat in Class

Chapter 4: A Hidden Talent

After the academic competition, something began to change at Green Valley Secondary School.

Students who once ignored Samuel started greeting him.

They had discovered that the quiet boy from the last seat was not only intelligent but also hardworking.

However, there was still something about Samuel that nobody knew.

Samuel had a talent he rarely showed to others.

Since he was young, he loved creating things with his hands.

While other children played outside, Samuel often collected old materials and turned them into useful objects.

Broken radios, pieces of wood, old wires, and empty bottles became tools for his imagination.

To him, every useless object had a hidden purpose.

One afternoon, the school announced a creativity exhibition.

Students were asked to present projects that could help solve problems in their community.

Many students prepared expensive projects using materials they bought from shops.

Samuel did not have money to buy new materials.

Instead, he looked around his home and collected what he could find.

For several nights, he worked quietly after finishing his homework.

He designed a simple device that could help farmers save water when watering crops.

It was made from recycled materials, but it worked.

When Samuel presented his invention at the exhibition, many students were surprised.

"This was made from old materials?" one student asked.

Samuel smiled.

"Sometimes the things people throw away still have value."

The judges carefully examined his project.

After a short discussion, they announced the results.

"First place goes to Samuel."

The hall became filled with applause.

Even Kevin, who once looked down on him, remained silent.

For the first time, he began to understand that Samuel's strength was not about money or appearance.

It was about creativity, determination, and believing in himself.

That day, Samuel learned something important:

A hidden talent can shine anywhere, even from the last seat in class.
