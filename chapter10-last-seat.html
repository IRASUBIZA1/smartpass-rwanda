📖 The Last Seat in Class

Chapter 10: The Final Test

Receiving the scholarship was one of the happiest moments of Samuel's life.

But he soon discovered that success came with greater responsibility.

The scholarship required him to maintain excellent grades and represent his school in national academic competitions.

Samuel knew that this was his chance to prove that his dreams were possible.

However, the pressure was greater than ever before.

Every day, he studied harder than before.

He woke up early, attended classes, helped his classmates, and spent evenings preparing for exams.

Sometimes, he felt exhausted.

Sometimes, he wondered if he could continue.

One evening, Samuel sat alone outside his home looking at the sky.

His father found him there.

"What are you thinking about?" his father asked.

Samuel replied:

"I am afraid of failing the people who believe in me."

His father sat beside him.

"Samuel, remember something. We are proud of you because of your effort, not only your results."

Those words gave Samuel peace.

The day of the national examination finally arrived.

Students from different schools gathered in a large examination centre.

Among them were some of the best students in the country.

Samuel looked around and remembered where he started.

The boy with the old school bag.

The boy sitting at the last seat.

The boy nobody noticed.

Now he was standing among the best students.

The examination began.

The questions were difficult.

Many students became nervous.

But Samuel stayed calm.

He used everything he had learned through years of hard work.

When the final paper ended, Samuel left the examination room with confidence.

He knew he had given his best.

Weeks later, the results were announced.

The entire school gathered to hear the news.

The head teacher held the results sheet.

"We are proud to announce that one of our students achieved one of the highest scores in the country."

Everyone waited.

"The student is Samuel."

The school celebrated.

Kevin smiled proudly.

The teachers congratulated him.

Samuel looked at his classmates and remembered his journey.

He learned that success was not about where someone started.

It was about the courage to continue.

The last seat in class had not been the end of his story.

It had been the beginning.
