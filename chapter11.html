📖 Beyond The Dusty Roads

Chapter 11: The Last Memory

Life seemed brighter than before.

For the first time in years, Linka had a true friend.

Although her situation had not changed completely, Adrian's presence gave her hope.

One afternoon, after returning from the market, Linka noticed something strange outside her aunt's house.

Several old clothes were missing.

Her heart immediately became uneasy.

She rushed inside and searched through the small wooden box where she kept the few belongings left by her mother.

The box was nearly empty.

The dresses, scarves, and other precious items her mother had left behind were gone.

Linka ran outside and found her aunt speaking with a trader.

"Where are my mother's clothes?" she asked, her voice shaking.

Her aunt looked at her without concern.

"I sold them."

For a moment, Linka could not believe what she had heard.

Those clothes were the last memories she had of her mother.

They were not valuable because of money.

They were valuable because of love.

Tears filled her eyes.

"How could you do that?" she cried.

Her aunt shrugged.

"I needed money."

Then she held out a small amount of cash.

"Now stop crying and buy me a drink. I'm thirsty."

The words cut deeper than any insult.

Linka stood there silently, unable to understand how someone could be so heartless.

That evening, she sat alone beneath the same tree where she often worked on her designs.

She cried until she had no tears left.

When Adrian found her, he immediately knew something was wrong.

After hearing what had happened, he sat beside her quietly.

For the first time, he saw just how much pain she had been carrying all these years.

And at that moment, he made a promise to himself.

No matter what happened, he would help Linka find a better future.
