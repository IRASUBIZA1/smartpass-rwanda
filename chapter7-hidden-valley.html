Chapter 7: The Enemy Who Wants Power

The ancient chamber became silent as Ethan faced Victor. The young student could feel fear inside him, but he refused to step away.

Victor walked closer to the Heart of Amani.

"You are only a child," Victor said. "You cannot understand the power that is here."

Ethan looked at him calmly.

"Maybe I am young, but I understand something you forgot."

Victor stopped.

"What?"

"Knowledge is not valuable because it makes one person powerful. It is valuable because it helps many people."

Elder Kato smiled.

"Now you understand the true lesson of Amani."

Victor became angry.

"You think kindness can defeat ambition?"

He reached toward the Heart of Amani.

Suddenly, the entire chamber began shaking.

The crystal changed its light and created a barrier around itself.

Victor stepped back in surprise.

"Why is it rejecting me?"

Elder Kato answered:

"Because the Heart of Amani does not respond to those who seek power only for themselves."

Victor looked at Ethan.

"Then you think you are worthy?"

Ethan shook his head.

"No. I don't think I am special."

He looked at the glowing crystal.

"But I know I want to use this knowledge to help others."

The Heart of Amani became brighter.

A hidden door appeared behind the chamber.

Maya looked amazed.

"Ethan... it opened for you."

Victor stepped back.

He realized something he had never understood.

The valley was not searching for the smartest person.

It was searching for someone with the right heart.

But Victor was not ready to give up.

"This is not over," he said.

"I will return."

He disappeared into the darkness of the valley.

Ethan, Maya, and Elder Kato entered the hidden room behind the crystal.

Inside was something nobody expected.

A message from the first guardians of Amani.

And it was written for Ethan.

"To the one who discovers this message: your journey has only begun."

Ethan looked at Maya.

They both knew that the secrets of the valley were much bigger than they imagined.
