📖 The Last Seat in Class

Chapter 11: The Boy From The Last Seat Becomes A Leader

After the national examination results were announced, Samuel became known throughout Green Valley Secondary School.

Many students admired his success, but Samuel did not allow fame to change him.

He was still the same student who arrived early, helped others, and respected everyone.

The head teacher noticed his character.

One morning, the head teacher called Samuel to his office.

"Samuel, we have watched your journey carefully," the head teacher said.

"You have shown that a true leader is not someone who is only successful, but someone who helps others succeed."

Samuel listened carefully.

"We would like you to become a student leader."

Samuel was surprised.

"Me? A leader?"

The head teacher smiled.

"Yes. Leadership is not about where you come from. It is about the impact you make."

Samuel accepted the responsibility.

As a student leader, he decided to focus on helping students who struggled like he once did.

He started a study group where students could learn together after classes.

He encouraged students who felt that they were not good enough.

He told them his own story.

"I was once the student sitting alone at the back of the classroom. Never believe that your situation defines your future."

Many students found courage through his words.

Even Kevin became part of the study group.

Together, they helped other students improve their performance.

One afternoon, a young student approached Samuel.

"I used to think I could never succeed because my family is poor," the student said.

Samuel smiled.

"I thought the same thing once. But your beginning does not decide your ending."

Those words became a message throughout the school.

The student who once sat at the last seat had become someone who gave hope to others.

Samuel finally understood something important:

Real success is not reaching the top alone. It is helping others rise with you.

But Samuel's journey was not finished yet.

A bigger dream was waiting beyond the walls of Green Valley Secondary School.
