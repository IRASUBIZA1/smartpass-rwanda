Chapter 7: The Final Argument

The peaceful gathering under the mango tree suddenly became tense when an angry voice interrupted the meeting.

It was Mr. Kamali, one of the oldest members of the village and a person who had always supported the family conflict.

"You cannot erase years of pain with one letter," he said.

The people became silent.

Amina looked at him calmly.

"We are not trying to erase the past. We are trying to learn from it."

Mr. Kamali shook his head.

"You are young. You do not understand what happened."

David stepped forward.

"Maybe we do not know everything, but we know that hatred has not solved anything."

The crowd started whispering.

Some people agreed with David. Others still believed the old story.

Mr. Kamali brought out an old document.

"This is proof that we were right all along," he said.

Everyone looked surprised.

Amina carefully examined the document.

She noticed something strange.

"The date on this paper does not match the year of the conflict," she said.

The crowd became curious.

David looked closer.

"This document was created after the disagreement started."

A silence filled the village.

For the first time, people began questioning the story they had believed for years.

Mr. Kamali became nervous.

"The truth is more complicated than you think," he said.

Amina replied:

"Then let us discover the whole truth together instead of continuing to fight."

The argument ended without a final answer.

But something important had changed.

People were no longer blindly following the past.

They were finally searching for the truth.

And the biggest secret of the family conflict was still waiting to be revealed.
