Chapter 3: The First Journey

The following Saturday morning, Ethan and Maya prepared for their journey. They packed some food, water, notebooks, and the mysterious map.

They did not know what they would find, but they knew one thing: they had to discover the truth about the Hidden Valley of Amani.

"Are you sure we should do this?" Maya asked while looking at the tall mountains ahead.

Ethan smiled.

"Sometimes the biggest discoveries begin with a little courage."

The two friends followed the path shown on the map. At first, the journey seemed easy. They walked through green fields and crossed small rivers.

But as they moved closer to the mountains, the path became harder to follow.

The map showed a road that no longer existed.

"We might be lost," Maya said.

Ethan looked around carefully.

"No. Look at the trees."

On several trees, they found the same symbol from the old book — the tree with deep roots.

Someone had left signs behind.

They continued following the symbols until they reached a large stone wall covered with plants.

In the middle of the wall was a small entrance.

Above it were words carved into the stone:

"Only those who seek knowledge with a pure heart may enter."

Maya touched the words carefully.

"What do you think this means?"

Before Ethan could answer, the ground shook slightly.

The stone door slowly began to open.

Behind it was a hidden path leading deep into the mountain.

Ethan and Maya looked at each other.

They knew there was no turning back.

They stepped inside.

The moment they entered, the door closed behind them.

Darkness surrounded them.

Then suddenly, small lights began appearing on the walls, showing ancient drawings of people learning, creating, and helping others.

"This place is incredible," Maya whispered.

But before they could explore further, they heard a voice coming from the darkness.

"Who has entered the Hidden Valley of Amani?"

Ethan and Maya froze.

Someone was waiting for them.
