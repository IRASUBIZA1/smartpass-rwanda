📖 The Last Seat in Class

Chapter 3: The First Challenge

After Samuel solved the difficult mathematics problem, many students started noticing him.

Some respected him, but others were not happy about the attention he was receiving.

Among them was Kevin, one of the most popular students in the class.

Kevin was known for having expensive clothes, a new phone, and many friends around him.

He believed that students from poor backgrounds could never compete with him.

"Solving one question does not make you special," Kevin told Samuel one afternoon.

Samuel looked at him calmly.

"I am not trying to be special. I am just trying to learn."

Kevin laughed.

"People like you should know your place."

Samuel did not answer.

He remembered his mother's words:

"Never let someone else decide your value."

A few days later, the school announced an academic competition.

Students from different classes would compete in mathematics, science, and general knowledge.

The winner would receive a scholarship and represent the school at a national competition.

The teacher selected Samuel as one of the participants.

When students heard the news, everyone started talking.

"Samuel? The new boy?"

"Can he really compete against the best students?"

Samuel heard the comments, but he remained focused.

Every evening after school, he studied harder.

He used old books, borrowed notes, and practiced many questions.

At home, his parents noticed how much effort he was putting in.

"We are proud of you," his mother told him.

"Winning is not everything. What matters is giving your best."

The day of the competition finally arrived.

Students filled the school hall as the challenge began.

Samuel sat among students who had more resources, better equipment, and more confidence.

But he had something powerful:

Determination.

As the first question appeared on the screen, Samuel took a deep breath.

The biggest challenge of his school journey had begun.
